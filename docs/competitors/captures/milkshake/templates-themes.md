# Milkshake — templates & themes

## Template architecture

Milkshake is **card-based** — the template isn't a static layout but
a **stack of cards** where each card has its own layout / purpose:
- About card
- Links card
- Recommendations card
- Social card
- Portfolio card
- Media kit card
- Shop card (display-only)
- Custom card

Creators assemble a page by selecting which card types they need and
customising each.

## Customisation controls

- **Colors** — per-card or page-level
- **Fonts** — limited set [unclear — exact count]
- **Logos / images** — uploaded by creator
- **"Shake it up"** — randomiser that generates a new colour + font +
  layout combo; creators "shake" until they like a result
- **Card reordering** — drag to reorder
- **Card hiding** (Pro+) — conditional display

## Template selection

- Milkshake opens onto a template picker
- Templates are combinations of pre-filled cards
- [unclear — exact template count; reviewer articles don't specify]

## Customisation ceiling

- **Shallow customisation** per reviewer critique — only colors +
  fonts + content are adjustable; no custom CSS, no arbitrary
  layouts, no font uploads
- **No multi-page structure** beyond cards
- **No interactive elements** beyond cards (no forms on free tier?
  [unclear])

## What makes Milkshake visually distinctive

- Card-based swipe interaction feels like Instagram Stories
- Mobile-first design with large tap targets
- Designer-level visual polish even on minimal effort
- Card-based design pattern is copyable but no competitor has
  adopted it at scale — still Milkshake's signature
