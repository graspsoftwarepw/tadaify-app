# Milkshake — reviews synthesis

## Rating snapshot

- **Apple App Store: 4.9/5 from 70K+ reviews** [source: direct fetch
  milkshake.app] — **category high**
- **5M+ downloads** (iOS + Android combined) [source: direct fetch]
- **Capterra: 4.6/5 from 290+ reviews** [source:
  https://www.creator-hero.com/blog/milkshake-vs-linktree-features-pricing-which-one-is-best-for-you]
- **G2: 4.3/5 from 210+ reviews** [source: same]
- Press features: Webby Awards, Vogue Codes, Lifehacker, InVision

Milkshake has the **best consumer-sentiment metrics in the research
set**. The 4.9 app store rating is a genuine moat.

## Top 5 praised features

1. **Ease of use on mobile** — "It's pretty awesome. The ease of use
   and functionality are so fun" [source: Creator Hero reviewer
   aggregate]

2. **Free tier is fully functional** — "completely free-of-cost plan
   that comes with all features, such as customizability, more link
   additions, and advanced analytics"
   [source: creator-hero.com/blog/milkshake-vs-linktree]

3. **Card-based visual design** — "Milkshake offers a unique
   opportunity to customize a card-based designed website where you
   can include about pages, recommendations, social media platforms,
   portfolio sites, and media kits"
   [source: same]

4. **Mobile-first speed** — "build your entire page from your phone
   in under 15 minutes" [source:
   https://limey.io/blog/linktree-vs-milkshake]

5. **Beautiful by default** — most Milkshake pages look
   professionally designed without creator effort

## Top 5 complaints

1. **No web version** — "Milkshake has no web version of the landing
   page builder; instead, it offers a dedicated app available in the
   App Store and Google Play" [source: Creator Hero reviewer aggregate]
   — for desktop-primary creators this is a dealbreaker

2. **Shallow customisation** — "Milkshake isn't that great in terms
   of customization; the only adjustments you can make, aside from
   content, are changing colors and fonts" [source: same]

3. **No native commerce** — "Milkshake has a 'shop' card, but you
   can't process orders and payments on the platform" [source:
   limey.io/blog/linktree-vs-milkshake]

4. **Linktree wins on monetisation** — "Linktree wins on
   monetization—it supports direct product sales, tip jars, and
   payment links" [source: same]

5. **Reviewer wish: more paid-tier features** — "I would love to
   have a few more features added, even if to the paid subscription"
   [source: reviewer quote]

## Churn / cancellation reasons

- Creators who want native commerce migrate to Stan / Beacons
- Desktop-primary creators never adopt
- Power users who hit customisation ceiling migrate to Carrd or
  Webflow

## Pricing complaints

- Minimal — Lite $2.99 is the cheapest branding-removal in the
  category
- Some annoyance about App Store subscription management (standard
  iOS UX, not Milkshake-specific)

## Feature requests

- **Web version** of the editor (most-requested)
- Native commerce / payments
- More customisation depth (custom fonts, layouts)
- Public API
- Shopify native integration
