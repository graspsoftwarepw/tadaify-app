# Linktree — third-party user reviews synthesis

## Rating snapshot (April 2026)

- **Capterra**: 4.4 / 5 based on 107 verified reviews [source:
  https://www.capterra.com/p/229171/Linktree/reviews/]
- **G2**: review surface active, star average aligns with Capterra
  [source: https://www.g2.com/products/linktree/reviews]
- **Product Hunt**: long legacy thread dating back to product launch
  [source: https://www.producthunt.com/products/linktree/reviews]
- **Google Play (Android app)**: Linktree Android app live under the
  "Linktree: Link in bio creator" listing
  [source: https://play.google.com/store/apps/details/Linktree_Link_in_bio_creator?id=ee.linktr.admin&hl=en_US]

## Top 5 praised features (with quote + source)

1. **Dead-simple onboarding** — "incredibly easy to use, making it a
   great choice for beginners, with setup taking just a few minutes"
   [source: Capterra review body quoted at
   https://www.capterra.com/p/229171/Linktree/reviews/]

2. **Generous free plan** — "Linktree is a free tool, and while paid
   plans are offered, the service doesn't exclude consumers that don't
   have the need for advanced features" [source: Capterra]

3. **Link-tracking / basic analytics** — "users appreciate the ability
   to track visits and clicks, which gives valuable insight into where
   leads originate" [source: Capterra]

4. **Preview before publishing** — "the ability to customize your link
   page and review how it'll be seen by visitors before publishing"
   [source: Capterra]

5. **Brand ubiquity of linktr.ee/<slug>** — "linktr.ee URL serving as a
   trusted, identifiable and familiar link that audiences feel
   comfortable clicking" [source:
   https://biotree.bio/blog/what-is-a-link-in-bio-page]

## Top 5 complained-about features (with quote + source)

1. **Content moderation takedowns without recourse** — "many Linktree
   users have reported that their accounts were taken down without any
   warning or explanation, and there is little to no support"
   [source:
   https://www.networksolutions.com/blog/linktree-alternatives/]
   Also: "Linktree reviews links within your linktree and if they don't
   approve of your content, your account gets locked up"
   [source: Capterra]

2. **Customer support is slow / bot-first** — "one user mentioned that
   when they tried starting a thread, a customer service bot was
   introduced that didn't go anywhere" [source: Capterra]
   Also: "Some users report being overcharged for years with no customer
   support, and Linktree refused to close accounts even with zero
   activity for over 2 years" [source: Capterra]

3. **No custom domain on ANY tier** — "Linktree doesn't offer custom
   domains on any plan—you're stuck with the linktr.ee/<username> URL"
   [source:
   https://www.networksolutions.com/blog/linktree-alternatives/]
   This is a SEO and brand complaint: "you don't own your landing page
   or control the underlying infrastructure"

4. **Design / customisation ceiling on free tier** — "users express
   complaints about limited control over the design aspect, with
   templates being nice but lacking the ability to design to match the
   brand" and "creating a fully branded appearance sometimes requires
   the paid plan" [source: Capterra]

5. **Confusing editor / too many clicks to add a link** — "some wish
   adding links were easier, citing too many buttons to click through
   to add a link" [source: Capterra]
   Also: "the user experience and interface can be a bit complicated
   and hard to use" [source:
   https://www.networksolutions.com/blog/linktree-alternatives/]

## Churn / cancellation reasons mentioned

- **Took-down my account over unclear policy** — primary churn trigger
  leading to Pallyy / Shorby / Beacons migrations [source:
  networksolutions, bloggingwizard, hopperhq]
- **Not worth the upgrade price for what I get** — users downgrading to
  free or leaving once they realise branding removal doesn't justify
  $9/mo (pre-Nov 2025) / $8/mo (post-increase)
- **Competitors have zero fees on free / custom domains on low tiers**
  — Shorby custom domains + Beacons lower fees drive defection
  [source: hopperhq.com]
- **Seller fee of 12% on free tier** is a large tax on anyone actually
  trying to monetise [source: talkspresso, biotree]

## Pricing complaints specifically

- **November 2025 price hike** — "Linktree raised prices across all
  paid plans in November 2025"; perceived as painful mid-year
  [source: talkspresso]
- **Premium at $35/mo is a big step up** — the zero-fee benefit only
  pays back at real sales volume; the gap between Pro and Premium feels
  punitive [source: creator-hero.com, khaby.ai]
- **Free tier 12% seller fee** — effectively a premium tax for hobby
  creators [source: talkspresso]

## Feature requests popping up repeatedly

- Custom domain at any tier [source: networksolutions, hopperhq,
  daily-ads]
- Lower seller fees on paid tiers (pressure to cut the 9% down)
  [source: biotree]
- Better content-moderation transparency / appeal process
  [source: capterra, bloggingwizard]
- Easier bulk link editing / drag-drop reorder [unclear — historically a
  complaint, may have been addressed]
- Bio-character-limit increase [source: Capterra — 80 chars flagged]
- First-party import from other link-in-bio tools [unclear — requested
  by users migrating OUT of other tools into Linktree]
- More typography / CSS control [source: Capterra]
- SEO meta fields per link [unclear — requested]
- Multi-language page versions [unclear]

## Reddit / community sentiment

- r/InstagramMarketing, r/CreatorsAdvice, r/SmallYouTubers frequently
  host threads on Linktree vs alternatives
- Sentiment in 2025-2026 trends negative on Linktree relative to 2022-
  2023 — the "original but bloated" narrative is visible
  [source: noupe.com/blog/linktree-alternatives,
  bloggingwizard.com/linktree-alternatives]
