# Bio.link (Hopp by Wix) — landing page audit

## Critical rebrand note

**`bio.link` was acquired and rebranded to `Hopp` (now "Hopp by Wix"
after Wix acquired Name.com's Hopp product line).** As of April 2026:
- Primary marketing lives at [hopp.bio/link-in-bio](https://www.hopp.bio/link-in-bio)
  and [name.com/hopp](https://www.name.com/hopp)
- Secondary hub: [hopp.co/blog](https://www.hopp.co/blog)
- The `bio.link` short domain is used as the profile URL template
  (`bio.link/<username>`) and as the .bio/.link branded-domain offer
- Wix help centre covers Hopp at
  [support.wix.com/en/article/hopp-by-wix](https://support.wix.com/en/article/hopp-by-wix-adding-and-managing-actions-for-link-in-bio)

All references to "bio.link" in this research file refer to the
current Hopp by Wix product.

## Hero positioning

> "The link in bio that's built for business"
>
> "Run your business right from your bio. Hopp's link sharing and
> monetization tools are built to streamline your online content and
> help your followers find exactly what they're looking for."

— [source: direct fetch of name.com/hopp]

This is the sharpest positioning angle in the category: Hopp targets
**business users** explicitly (freelancers, small brands, creators-as-
businesses) rather than "all creators". It's a deliberate anti-
Linktree stance.

## Primary CTAs

- **Log in and start for free** (appears multiple times on name.com/hopp)
- No prominent "upgrade" CTA on the main marketing page — pricing is
  downplayed in favour of feature exploration

## Value-prop blocks (from direct fetch)

1. **Get more traffic** — searchable-links feature so visitors can
   find specific products/services/coupon codes within the page
2. **Boost revenue** — ecommerce integration (Wix stores + Stripe)
3. **Increase loyalty** — email capture + contact forms
4. **Engage audience** — widgets (products, forms, email capture)

## Feature taxonomy (Hopp's vocabulary)

- "Link in bio"
- "Customizable search engine" (unique: on-page search over your own
  links — not surfaced by any other competitor in this research set)
- "Widgets" (products, forms, email capture)
- "Pre-rolls" (monetisation ad format — Hopp-native)
- "Short links" (branded URL shortener bundled with Pro)
- "Landing page builder" (mini landing-page tool)
- "Contact management" (basic CRM — import/export)

## Social proof

- Positioning under Wix's umbrella confers implicit trust (Wix = NYSE-
  listed website-builder, large brand)
- No specific "X million creators" number surfaced on Hopp landing
- [unclear — testimonials or logos list]

## Trust signals

- Backed by Wix (large-cap public company)
- Integrates with Stripe for payments [source:
  https://support.wix.com/en/article/hopp-by-wix- — Stripe settings
  article]
- Branded-domain offer (.bio / .link) included on Pro — you can own
  your URL

## Growth hooks

- **Free .bio or .link domain** included with Pro — most direct custom-
  domain offer in the category
- **Searchable-links engine** — unique feature that's a content-
  marketing hook
- **Pre-rolls (ad format)** — ownership / monetisation hook via Hopp-
  specific ad product
- Wix-ecosystem crossover — Wix users upgrade to Hopp add-on
- Name.com crossover — users buying a domain from Name.com funnel
  into Hopp

## CTAs direct observations

- Primary: "Log in and start for free"
- No mandatory credit card on free
- No "free trial" framing — it's a proper free-forever tier

## Segment landings

Hopp appears to run few segment-specific pages — its main
segmentation is "business vs personal" rather than "creators by
niche".
