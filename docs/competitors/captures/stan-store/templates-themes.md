# Stan Store — templates & themes scan

## Scope

Stan Store's theming surface is **narrowly scoped** — 11 theme
styles, 10 fonts, 2 colours — and this is a deliberate product
decision. Stan trades design flexibility for speed-to-launch and
conversion consistency (every Stan store looks "Stan-like" and
audiences develop cognitive familiarity with the pattern).

## Customisation controls (documented)

From [help.stan.store/article/180](https://help.stan.store/article/180-how-to-customize-your-store-fonts-and-colors):

- **11 theme styles** — preset style families
- **Fonts**: 10 options (mix of serif + sans-serif)
- **Colours**: 2 custom colours per store (hex input OR colour
  picker)
- **Header image**: 1 custom upload
- **Course-product-level overrides**: colours + banner image per
  course product [source:
  https://help.stan.store/article/247-how-to-customize-your-course-product]

## Customisation ceiling

What Stan does NOT support:
- Custom CSS / HTML
- Arbitrary number of colours (2-max)
- Self-hosted fonts / @font-face
- Per-product background overrides (beyond course products)
- Animated backgrounds
- Multiple pages (Stan is a single-page store by design)
- Custom domains [unclear — some reviewer sources suggest custom
  domain is supported; Stan's primary model is `stan.store/<slug>` but
  paid-tier custom-domain mapping may exist; needs live audit]

## Template gallery

Stan does NOT publish a public template gallery at a marketing URL.
However, the [help article 330 — Stan Store Examples](https://help.stan.store/article/330-stan-store-examples)
functions as a semi-public gallery organised by creator niche.

Third-party cottage industry:
- **Etsy** — "Stan Store Template" tag has sellers monetising
  pre-built Stan configurations (colour palettes + font pairings +
  sample product copy)
  [source: https://www.etsy.com/market/stan_store_template]
- **Reveal Studio** — paid Stan Store sales-page template
  [source: https://shoprevealstudioco.com/products/stan-store-sales-page-template]
- **TikTok how-tos** — "How to make my Stan Store look like a
  website" is a popular search
  [source: https://www.tiktok.com/discover/how-to-make-my-stan-store-look-like-a-website]

The existence of the third-party template market confirms two
things:
1. Stan's in-product customisation is inadequate for creators with
   design requirements
2. There's paying demand for differentiation (Etsy sellers charge
   $15-50 per Stan template configuration)

## Mobile preview

Stan is mobile-first by design — the editor's default preview IS
mobile. Desktop preview secondary. [unclear — whether desktop
