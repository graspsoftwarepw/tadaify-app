Logo motion v6 — The Step Forward

The creator steps slightly out from the centre of their stage, holds the position long enough for the audience to see them, then returns home. Most of the loop is stillness. The step alternates sides across cycles — so the logo reads as poised and present, never as pacing.

Why this works

Self-review

  • Reads as "creator on a stage" on first view. A single deliberate lean-and-hold is the universal body language for "I'm stepping forward to greet you". Pendulum sway reads as a metronome; v6 reads as a person.
  • Doesn't annoy on the 10th view. Motion occupies only ~4.3 s of a 14 s cycle — and the 2.2 s hold is static, so perceived stillness is closer to 12 s per loop. Over a 10-minute session the motion happens ~42 times but is barely registered.
  • Premium vs playful. No bounce, no elastic, no colour shift, no scale. One direction per cycle, ease-in-out-cubic (the Material emphasised-standard curve). The motion is the same language Apple and Linear use for sheet transitions — restrained and purposeful.

Honest tradeoffs

  • Sides alternate every 14 s — a viewer watching for less than 30 s will only see one direction, which asymmetrically biases the brand visually. Acceptable because the static mark is symmetric and dominates brand surfaces.
  • Less dynamic than v4. There is no "reveal" beat, no crescendo. On product pages with competing motion (video, carousel), v6 may read as pure stillness — which is by design but should be considered when placing next to louder content.
