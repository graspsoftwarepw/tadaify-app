What changed vs v5 / v6 / v7 delta

v8 introduces: full 360° orbit

  • Full orbit (new): creator travels the complete perimeter — none of v1-v7 had orbital motion. It's now genuinely going around the stage.
  • Still subtle: 5.6 s orbit duration feels deliberate and confident, not rushed. The sway on top (±3% radial breathing) is ornament — the orbit is the star.
  • Addresses the continuous-but-visible requirement: the entire 360° sweep is the "continuous" part; the gentle sway within it is the "present on stage" part.
  • v5 was "too slow" — v8 moves continuously for most of its cycle (6.4s out of 8.2s). Only 0.8s of stillness.
  • v6 was "too static" — v8 never pauses at the edge; it keeps walking the circle.
  • v7 was continuous L↔R sway — v8 replaces that linear pendulum with a circular orbit. The direction of travel is always changing → reads as circular, not back-and-forth.

Sway details Option A — radial breathing

Option A: Radial sway (selected)

During the orbit, the warm orb's distance from the stage center pulses ±3% of the orbit radius (18.4 SVG units). That's ±0.55 SVG units — barely perceptible, like a slow breath.

4 sway cycles per full 360° orbit. At 5.6 s for the orbit, one sway cycle = 1.4 s. Frequency ≈ 0.7 Hz — slow and gentle, well below the threshold for visual noise.

Analogy: an Apple Siri breathing orb that also happens to be walking in a circle. The sway says "I'm alive and present"; the orbit says "I'm making my rounds".

Accessibility

Reduced-motion support

  • @media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce) — all animation paused, warm orb stays centred.
  • Favicon sizes (32 px, 16 px) are always static — orbit is too small to read.
  • SVG has aria-label on animated logos; decorative ones use aria-hidden="true".

Open question for review

A/B: Orbit direction

Current (v8): Clockwise — right → down → left → up. Natural reading direction for LTR audiences; "going forward".

Alternative: Counter-clockwise — right → up → left → down. Slightly more theatrical / less corporate.

The orbit duration (5.6 s) feels comfortable but can be tightened to 4.4 s if you want a snappier feel, or loosened to 7 s for a more meditative pace. Current 5.6 s is the middle ground.
