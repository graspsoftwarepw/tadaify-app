# Pitch angles for tadaify outreach

> 10 concrete "pitch angles" tadaify could lead with when approaching tier-1 or tier-2 outlets. Each angle is structured as:
>
> 1. **Angle name** — short label
> 2. **Target outlet type** — which tier / which outlets
> 3. **One-paragraph pitch draft** — English, ready-to-adapt
> 4. **Proof / data tadaify needs** — what must be built/measured to back the claim

Pitches are ordered roughly from lowest-proof-required (can pitch at MVP launch) to highest-proof-required (needs months of usage data).

---

## Angle 1 — "The mockup-first link-in-bio" (design-led differentiation)

**Target outlet type**: Tier-1 design/creator publications (Site Builder Report, Creator Hero, Ecomm.design, Limey.io, Quinn Tempest). Tier-2 design-leaning bloggers. Design.Twitter / Design.reddit crossovers.

**Pitch draft**:
> Hi [name] — I'm the founder of tadaify. Unlike every other link-in-bio tool where creators are dumped into a WYSIWYG editor and asked to "design their page", tadaify ships with a mockup-first flow: you describe your brand in 3 sentences and we generate 4 visual mockups to pick from, then you edit. Every existing reviewer (including your 2024 piece on Carrd) notes that the "how do I make this NOT look generic" problem is real — we're solving it head-on. I'd love to send you a demo link + a lifetime Pro account. No embargo, no exclusive required — just an honest look.

**Proof tadaify needs**:
- AI-or-templated mockup generation that actually produces 4 distinct, brand-appropriate pages from a short brief.
- 10-20 beta-user pages demonstrating range.
- Optional: before/after pages showing the difference vs Linktree "set up in 5 minutes" default look.
- Time-to-first-good-page metric (we claim < 90 seconds vs industry average ~5 min).

## Angle 2 — "Post-Linkpop Shopify-native"

**Target outlet type**: Tier-1 ecommerce (Shopify Blog, ReferralCandy, Fourthwall). Shopify App Store, Shopify Partners blog.

**Pitch draft**:
> Hi [Shopify editor] — when Shopify sunset Linkpop on July 7, 2025, thousands of merchants were left choosing between bitLink, POWR, LinkShop, and Lnk.Bio — none of which feel Shopify-native. tadaify launches with a native Shopify app: live product sync, Shop Pay checkout inside the bio page, inventory-aware "sold out" badges, and collection-level embeds. Beta merchants: [X names]. GMV driven in first 30 days: [$Y]. Would you consider a tadaify inclusion in your next "Linktree alternatives" refresh — or, better, a standalone "best link-in-bio for Shopify 2026" piece? Happy to brief you on data.

**Proof tadaify needs**:
- SHIPPED Shopify app with at least: product sync (live), Shop Pay, collection embeds, analytics export to Shopify Admin.
- 30-50 beta merchants using it.
- GMV driven attributable to tadaify in aggregate (even $50k in first month is citable).
- Side-by-side comparison vs bitLink and Lnk.Bio (neutral tester).

## Angle 3 — "Link-in-bio for newsletter creators" (underserved niche)

**Target outlet type**: beehiiv blog, Kit (ConvertKit) resources, Substack creator guides, The Publish Press newsletter. Tier-2 creator-econ.

**Pitch draft**:
> Hi [beehiiv / Kit editor] — every link-in-bio tool optimizes for Instagram-first creators. But the 400k+ newsletter writers on beehiiv, Kit, and Substack are optimizing for a completely different funnel: social → bio page → **email signup**, not → purchase. tadaify ships with a newsletter-first bio page template: primary CTA is email capture, secondary is "Read last 3 issues", and we pre-connect to beehiiv / Kit / Substack via OAuth in < 30 seconds. Comparison beta with 20 newsletter writers showed [X]% higher email signup conversion vs Linktree. Would you run an editorial piece on "link-in-bio done right for newsletter creators"? I can give you the raw A/B data.

**Proof tadaify needs**:
- beehiiv / Kit / Substack OAuth integrations (ship at least 2 of 3 at launch).
- A newsletter-first bio template (primary CTA = email signup, auto-embed of latest 3 posts).
- A/B test with 15-20 real newsletter creators showing email-signup lift vs Linktree.
- Blog post on tadaify.com/for/newsletters with case study.

## Angle 4 — "Honest benchmarks — tadaify tested against 10 competitors" (data-journalism pitch)

**Target outlet type**: Tier-1 reviewers who respect neutral data (Site Builder Report, Zapier, ClickUp, IMH). Substack creator-econ analysts (New Economies, Ollie Forsyth).

**Pitch draft**:
> Hi [Site Builder Report / Ollie] — I noticed your "We tested 20 link-in-bio tools" is still the most-cited piece in the category. Two years after, prices and features have shifted dramatically. As part of our launch research, we benchmarked tadaify against the 10 biggest players on a standardized rubric: page load time (Google Lighthouse), click-through rate on a standard 5-link page, transaction-fee math for a creator earning $5k/mo, mobile-Safari layout shifts. We're happy to share the raw CSV + methodology with you — regardless of whether tadaify wins or loses on every metric. Would you be interested in a co-authored data piece?

**Proof tadaify needs**:
- Actual benchmarking dataset across 10 competitors (Lighthouse performance, fee math, mobile CLS, feature-checkbox grid).
- Methodology document a neutral reviewer can audit.
- Willingness to publish results even when tadaify loses on metrics (this builds trust).
- Raw CSV downloadable from tadaify.com/research.

**Proof tadaify needs**:

- Build-log / changelog public (e.g. tadaify.com/changelog).
- Stack + architecture one-pager willing to share.
- Founder(s) who will write openly (or guest podcast).

## Angle 7 — "For creators who hate transaction fees" (competitive positioning)

**Target outlet type**: Creator monetization blogs (The Leap, Stan blog community, Creator Economy Report, Passionfroot blog). Tier-2 indie creator blogs.

**Pitch draft**:
> Hi [editor] — most of your readers are creators earning between $1k and $10k/mo through link-in-bio storefronts. At that scale, the 9% Beacons transaction fee costs $90-900/mo — more than the $30 paid plan that removes it. Stan charges $29-99/mo with 0% fees. Linktree Shops takes brand commissions. tadaify is launching at **0% transaction fees on all paid plans starting at $15/mo** — cheapest honest-priced option in the category. We've built a "fee calculator" at tadaify.com/fee-calc so creators can compute their real cost on each tool. Would you consider a "cost of using a link-in-bio tool in 2026" analysis piece? We'll plug in every competitor, not just ourselves.

**Proof tadaify needs**:
- Actual $15/mo + 0% fee plan (pricing must hold).
- tadaify.com/fee-calc — interactive comparison calculator (MVP tier, 30 lines of JS).
- Clear fee-comparison chart updated monthly.
- Case study: 1-2 creators switching from Beacons/Stan to tadaify with math.

## Angle 10 — "We deleted 12 features Linktree has, and our pages load 3x faster" (contrarian performance pitch)

**Target outlet type**: Dev / engineering-curious outlets (Zapier, Hacker News, Smashing Magazine, Site Builder Report). Performance-obsessed creator blogs.

**Pitch draft**:
> Hi [Zapier editor] — tadaify is the opposite bet on link-in-bio. Every competitor has added hundreds of "features" that 98% of users ignore — cluttering the UI and slowing pages. tadaify shipped v1 with 12 features: the essentials. Mobile Safari First Contentful Paint: 0.6s (Linktree's average is 2.8s on the same test). Mobile-Safari Cumulative Layout Shift: 0.01 (Linktree: 0.18). We wrote an engineering post at tadaify.com/engineering/performance with the Lighthouse runs + exact CDN architecture (Cloudflare edge + Bunny.net images + SSR-on-deploy). Open-source the test harness at github.com/tadaify/bench. Would a Zapier writer like to cover the "less-features" thesis? Happy to demo.

**Proof tadaify needs**:
- Actual Lighthouse performance meaningfully better than Linktree (publishable numbers).
- Open-source benchmarking harness on GitHub.
- Engineering blog post with architecture details.
- Willing to be roasted if a reviewer finds we're slower on some dimension.

## Angle prioritization for first 90 days

- **Can pitch at MVP launch** (proof is light): Angle 1 (mockup-first), Angle 5 (indie SaaS in public), Angle 7 (0% fees).
- **Can pitch within 60-90 days of launch**: Angle 3 (newsletter creators), Angle 8 (niche verticals), Angle 10 (performance).
- **Requires Shopify app ship**: Angle 2.
- **Requires 3-6 months of aggregate data**: Angle 4 (benchmarks), Angle 6 (TC/Marketing Brew funding hook), Angle 9 (network).

**Recommended day-90 outreach mix**:
- 5 Angle 1 pitches → Site Builder Report, Creator Hero, Limey, Ecomm.design, Clickydrip
- 3 Angle 5 pitches → Indie Hackers diary, Starter Story, MicroConf
- 3 Angle 7 pitches → The Leap, Passionfroot blog, Creator Economy Report
- 5 Angle 8 niche pitches → Pixieset (photographers), NotNoise (musicians), liinks (real estate), beehiiv (newsletters), Kajabi (coaches)

Expected hit-rate: 20-30% response; 5-10% published piece. That gives us 2-4 T2 pieces in the first 90 days — enough social proof to then pitch T1s.
